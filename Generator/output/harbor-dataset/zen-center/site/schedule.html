IV — Annual rhythms.

The year at the hall is shaped by three observances. Ango — a ninety-day practice period — runs from mid-August through mid-November. Rohatsu, the December sesshin commemorating Shakyamuni's awakening, is the deepest week of the year. Founder's day, in early October, marks Daiun Soshin's death and the formal renewal of teaching roles.

Between these we hold smaller zazenkai, family practice mornings, and an annual one-day work retreat in spring, when the roof tiles are washed.

How to apply — for a residential retreat.

The application is six questions, one to a screen. We read each application by hand. Beginners are welcome and need not have prior experience; we ask only that you have sat through at least one half-day before signing on for a sesshin.

If the suggested dana band is a barrier, please write us. We hold a modest scholarship fund and would rather have you here than not.

What to expect

  • — Arrival the evening before; bedding provided.
  • — Phones surrendered at the genkan, returned on the closing day.
  • — Silence held throughout, except in dokusan and at the closing meal.
  • — Plain robes provided; bring only what fits in a small bag.
  • — Vegetarian meals; allergies noted on the form.
