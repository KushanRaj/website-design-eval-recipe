Listening notes — how to receive a dharma talk.

A teisho is not a lecture. It is the teacher's reading of an old text or story, given to the room that is sitting. There is no need to take notes; the talk is meant to enter sideways. If a passage strikes you, return to the recording the next morning at the time you would otherwise sit. The weather of the talk often changes a second time.
