The line we keep, and the teachers who keep it.

Pine Tree Hall is a small Sōtō Zen line descended from the thirteenth-century ancestor Dōgen Kigen by way of Keizan Jōkin. We belong to the Shōgaku-ji sub-line, which crossed to North America in 1962 with our founding teacher, Daiun Soshin. The chart below is genealogical, not hagiographic — a record of who studied with whom, ordained when, and to what role they presently hold.

A note on transmission

In our line, dharma transmission (denbō) is the formal acknowledgement of an ancestor-student bond and the authorization to ordain priests in turn. It is not certification of insight, nor a measure of one's practice. Many devoted practitioners are not transmitted; some who have received transmission spend the rest of their lives in obscurity. Reading the chart accordingly may help.

A note from the abbot.

The names above are not honors; they are tasks. A teacher in this line is someone who has agreed, in front of the community, to be available for the ordinary work of a monastery — answering letters, repairing roofs, listening well in the small hours. If you wish to study with one of them, please write rather than call, and allow a month for reply.

— Genshin
