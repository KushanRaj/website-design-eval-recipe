布施 — Dana The first of the six pāramitās Tax-receipt available

Dana sustains the hall.

We do not charge for teaching. The hall — the roof, the rice, the heat in the bath, the stipends for the resident teachers — is held aloft entirely by the gifts of those who find their practice nourished here. The Japanese word fuse (布施), the Pali dāna, names not a transaction but a way of being in the world: giving without expectation, receiving without owing.

Below are three paths for sustaining the hall, with suggested bands. Set whatever feels true for you. There is no minimum and no record kept of who gives what.

— from a recent talk by Genshin

Rice and oil 米油 — monthly recurring

Monthly giving is the most useful kind, because it lets the kitchen plan. The fund covers food, utilities, and small repairs — the daily, unremarkable expenses of a working monastery. Most lay members choose this path.

Roof restoration 屋根 — one-time capital gift

The cedar-shake roof of the Buddha Hall is forty-one years old and letting in the rain. We are quietly raising the cost of its full replacement over three years. Gifts to this fund are kept separate and reported on at the end of each ango.

Raised to date: $84,200 of $180,000.

Or a free amount $

Retreatant scholarship 勧学 — for those who cannot pay full dana

This fund holds places in residential retreats for those who cannot meet the suggested dana band. It is administered confidentially by the tanto. Requests are received without explanation; the fund is replenished by gifts here.

A practical note.

Pine Tree Hall is a registered religious nonprofit (501(c)(3)). Gifts are tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law. Receipts are sent by post in January for the prior year, or on request to treasurer@pinetreezen.org.

We do not accept cryptocurrency or in-kind gifts of stock without prior conversation. If you wish to remember the hall in a will, the treasurer can provide the appropriate language.
