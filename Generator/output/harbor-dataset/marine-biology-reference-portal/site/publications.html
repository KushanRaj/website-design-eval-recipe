Population genetics of the great white shark Carcharodon carcharias inferred from mitochondrial DNA sequences
VU Chondrichthyes OA
Pardini A.T. et al. Molecular Ecology 2023 182
We analyzed mitochondrial DNA sequences from 131 white sharks (Carcharodon carcharias) sampled from the Pacific, Atlantic, and Indian Oceans. Phylogeographic analyses revealed strong population structure between ocean basins, with low within-basin diversity consistent with historically small effective population sizes. Our data suggest that white shark populations are not panmictic globally and that conservation management units should be defined at the ocean-basin level.

Long-term decline in humpback whale feeding aggregations in the North Atlantic correlates with zooplankton biomass reduction
LC Mammalia
Stevenson C.F. et al. Marine Mammal Science 2022 94
Using 28 years of aerial survey data (1994–2022) from the Gulf of Maine feeding ground, we demonstrate a significant (p < 0.001) long-term decline in humpback whale (Megaptera novaeangliae) aggregation size that correlates strongly with reductions in Calanus finmarchicus copepod biomass. We attribute the copepod decline to sea surface temperature increases of 1.4°C over the study period, consistent with climate projections for the Northwest Atlantic Shelf.

Mixed-stock analysis of Atlantic bluefin tuna (Thunnus thynnus) at feeding grounds reveals asymmetric contributions from Mediterranean and Gulf of Mexico spawning stocks
EN Actinopterygii OA
Rooker J.R. et al. ICES J. Mar. Science 2022 156
We applied otolith δ¹³C–δ¹⁸O isotope analysis and archival tagging to characterize stock mixing of Atlantic bluefin tuna in the Northwest Atlantic. Results indicate that approximately 64% of the sampled individuals originated from Mediterranean spawning grounds, with the remainder attributable to the Gulf of Mexico stock. Stock mixing ratios varied significantly between years (2018–2022), suggesting dynamic and environment-dependent mixing that complicates single-stock management assumptions.

A global assessment of whale shark (Rhincodon typus) abundance using photo-identification and mark-recapture analysis
EN Chondrichthyes OA
Peel L.R. et al. Endang. Species Res. 2021 231
We combined photo-identification records (n = 7,212 individuals) from Wildbook for Whale Sharks with satellite telemetry data to produce the first global abundance estimate for whale sharks. Using a Cormack-Jolly-Seber open-population model, we estimate the global population at approximately 180,000 (95% CI: 120,000–290,000) individuals. Population trend analysis indicates a 63% decline over three generations (approximately 75 years), confirming the species' IUCN Endangered status.

Vertical migration patterns and thermoregulatory basking behaviour in ocean sunfish (Mola mola) in temperate waters
VU Actinopterygii
Nakamura I. et al. Deep-Sea Research I 2021 67
Archival pop-up satellite tags deployed on 24 ocean sunfish in the northwestern Pacific revealed consistent daily vertical migration from epipelagic surface waters to mesopelagic depths of up to 844 m. Deep dives occurred primarily during daylight hours and were followed by extended surface basking, supporting the thermoregulatory hypothesis. Mean dive duration was 26 minutes, with deeper dives in warmer surface conditions, suggesting active thermoregulation to maintain optimal core temperatures for neural and locomotory function.

Convergent evolution of killer whale (Orcinus orca) ecotypes revealed by comparative genomics
DD Mammalia
Foote A.D. et al. Current Biology 2023 298
High-coverage whole-genome resequencing of 48 killer whales spanning all known ecotypes revealed that dietary and ecological divergence has driven convergent evolution across ocean basins. Antarctic fish-eating and marine mammal-eating ecotypes show independent evolution of sensory and digestive gene variants. Demographic modelling places ecotype divergence times at 100,000–200,000 years ago, predating or coinciding with the Last Glacial Maximum, with strong evidence for selection on MHC and olfactory receptor gene clusters.

Critically Endangered status of the scalloped hammerhead (Sphyrna lewini): bycatch rates, CPUE trends, and nursery habitat loss in Atlantic coastal systems
CR Chondrichthyes OA
Duncan K.M. et al. Mar. Ecol. Prog. Ser. 2020 142
We synthesized 40 years of CPUE data from Atlantic longline fisheries alongside acoustic telemetry of 187 individuals to assess population status and habitat use of the scalloped hammerhead in the western North Atlantic. Standardized CPUE declined by 89% (1980–2019). Nursery site fidelity was high, and three of eight identified coastal nursery habitats have been lost to estuarine development since 1990. We urge emergency bycatch mitigation measures and legal protection of remaining nursery estuaries.

Decadal acoustic monitoring of blue whale (Balaenoptera musculus) call frequency shift in the North Pacific: evidence for vocal learning or population recovery?
EN Mammalia
Širovic A. et al. J. Acoust. Soc. Am. 2023 88
Passive acoustic recordings from 12 hydrophone arrays in the North Pacific (2004–2023) document a steady decline in blue whale call frequency of approximately 0.3 Hz per year, a trend observed globally. Two competing hypotheses—ambient noise masking and post-whaling population recovery—were tested using noise level co-variates and population density estimates. Our analysis supports neither hypothesis exclusively, but is most consistent with cultural transmission of call patterns across a slowly recovering population in which density-dependent effects are beginning to emerge.

Bycatch of loggerhead (Caretta caretta) and leatherback (Dermochelys coriacea) sea turtles in Mediterranean fisheries: updated estimates and mitigation priorities
VU Reptilia OA
Casale P. et al. Biological Conservation 2021 204
We synthesized observer programme data and fishing effort statistics from 17 Mediterranean countries (2010–2019) to update sea turtle bycatch estimates. Annual bycatch of loggerhead turtles is estimated at 43,000–80,000 individuals, of which 8–15% are expected to be lethal interactions. Leatherback bycatch, previously underestimated, is concentrated in pelagic longline fleets and may represent a key driver of the species' poor conservation status in the Mediterranean basin.

Population connectivity and site fidelity of giant manta rays (Mobula birostris) across Indo-Pacific aggregation sites
VU Chondrichthyes OA
Stewart J.D. et al. R. Soc. Open Sci. 2020 119
Photo-identification of 3,812 individual giant manta rays at 22 aggregation sites across the Indo-Pacific (2010–2019) revealed high site fidelity and limited inter-site connectivity. Bayesian network analysis identified three largely independent population clusters (East Indian Ocean, Coral Triangle, Central Pacific) separated by genetic FST values of 0.11–0.22. These findings suggest that site-specific threats—particularly targeted fisheries and gill-raker trade—can have disproportionate regional impacts even on a globally Vulnerable species.

Genetic structure and cryptic diversity within the short-snouted seahorse complex (Hippocampus hippocampus sensu lato) across the European Atlantic
LC Actinopterygii
Woodall L.C. et al. Zool. J. Linn. Soc. 2019 58
Multi-locus microsatellite and mitochondrial DNA analysis of 840 seahorse specimens from 26 Atlantic coastal sites reveals cryptic genetic divergence within the nominal species Hippocampus hippocampus. Three genetically distinct clades correspond to geographical barriers imposed by the Almeria-Oran front and the English Channel. Morphological differentiation is subtle but detectable in rostral length-to-height ratios. We recommend these lineages be re-evaluated as candidate species.

Age structure, growth parameters, and reproductive biology of skipjack tuna (Katsuwonus pelamis) in the Western and Central Pacific Ocean
LC Actinopterygii OA
Murua H. et al. Fisheries Research 2024 12
Otolith thin-sections from 2,847 skipjack tuna collected in WCPFC observer programme samples were used to estimate age, growth (von Bertalanffy parameters: L∞ = 82.3 cm, K = 0.79 yr⁻¹), and reproductive season length. Modal age at first spawning was 1.6 years.
