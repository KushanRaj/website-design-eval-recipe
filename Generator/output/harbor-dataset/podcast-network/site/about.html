Founding timeline

A short
nine-year arc.

Eight founding moments — drawn as a vertical waveform with one year-mark per node.

2017 · Founded

Frequency Room is named.

Reza Almasi and Petra Houx record the first three episodes of Static Hum on a kitchen table in Greenpoint. The name "Frequency Room" appears in a notebook on the third Sunday.

2018 · First taping

The Pencil Factory taping.

The first live taping of Static Hum happens in front of forty-eight people at the Pencil Factory in Greenpoint. We sell out by accident.

2019 · Two shows

Skeleton Hours debuts at 2 AM.

Vesper Lane joins the network. Skeleton Hours is recorded entirely between 02:00 and 04:00 for its first season.

2020 · The basement studio

We move into Studio B.

The network signs a long lease on a sub-basement on Calyer Street. We name the booth Studio B and never get around to naming a Studio A.

2022 · Co-op

The audio co-op is formed.

Frequency Room reorganizes as a worker-owned audio co-op. Every host, producer and engineer holds shares. We publish the first version of the manifesto.

2023 · Inner Circle

Late Pass and Skeleton Key launch.

Member tiers go live. We promise — in writing — never to raise the price for legacy members. We have not.

2024 · The Late Signal

The Late Signal joins as Channel 03.

Mara Quan and August Vellor pitch the show via the front-door pitch form. We say yes within six hours.

2026 · Twelve channels

Wet Concrete is announced.

The twelfth channel is announced for August 2026. We commit to capping the network at fifteen resident shows, ever.
