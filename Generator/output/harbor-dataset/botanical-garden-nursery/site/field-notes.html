Field Note · No. 41 · The Late-Spring Number

Why the Blue Poppy Mists in May, and Sulks in July

by Helena Mór, Director of the Conservatory

Of all the imports we have attempted to grow on the wet side of the Cascades, none has educated me more than this poppy from Tibet.

It took thirty-two years to learn that the Tsangpo blue poppy, Meconopsis betonicifolia, wants three things in equal proportion — cool, water, and patience — and that the gardener supplies only the first two. The third, given long enough, supplies itself.
