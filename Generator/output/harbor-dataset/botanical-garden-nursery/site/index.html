Field Notes & Cultivation
Cultivation · Cold-House

Meconopsis betonicifolia

The Himalayan Blue Poppy

The fabled blue poppy of the Tsangpo Gorge keeps to its Olympic kin like a homesick traveller. We sow in cold frames at first frost, never let it dry, and reward it with summer mist. Read the field note ↗

Heritage Collection

Camellia japonica 'Ostuyama'

A 1931 introduction from Kurume

One of forty-seven cultivars in the Camellia Glade, brought here from the founder's correspondence with Sakamoto Bunjirō. It still flowers more reliably than its grafted descendants. Read more ↗

Summer Cutting Beds

Lilium auratum 'Gold Band'

The Mountain Lily of Honshū

Eight feet, twelve buds, and the powdered perfume of a railway carriage in August. Grown in our cutting beds and sold as two-year bulbs by autumn dispatch. Reserve a bulb ↗
