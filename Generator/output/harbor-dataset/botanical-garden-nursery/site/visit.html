II.

What You'll See This Week

Updated by our curatorial team each Friday
Under Glass

The Orchid Bench

Three flowering Paphiopedilum fairrieanum, four Dendrobium nobile, and the first buds on Bulbophyllum lobbii. Glasshouse III is your destination.

Outdoors

Woodland Walk

Trillium ovatum and Erythronium revolutum carpet the path; the Helleborus × hybridus 'Slate Pearl' is at the top of its run.

Major Display

Rhododendron Dell

The tree-rhododendrons are at full truss: R. sinogrande, R. macabeanum, and the entire western face of R. arboreum.

III.

Accessibility & Comforts

For every visitor
Mobility

Step-Free Routes

All three glasshouses, the welcome pavilion, the propagation yard, and the Camellia Glade loop are paved and step-free. The Alpine Bank and Stone Garden include stepped paths with handrails; we will gladly route you around them.

Refreshments

Café Hortus

Open 9.30 – 16.30. A short menu of soups, ploughman's plates, and the conservatory's own honey tea. Outdoor seating on the herb terrace, weather permitting (always).

For Families

Small Visitors

Children under 12 are admitted free. Our Saturday Botanist programme runs 10.00 – 11.30 every weekend, ages 6 and up. The path map includes a child's seek-and-find route.

Visit On a Member's Card

Members enter free, with guests, every day we are open — and pay nothing for the late Thursdays, the Saturday Botanist sessions, or the open propagation hours. Join the household tier and bring the children.
