Varnish No. III — ‘Cremonese Amber’

A slow oil varnish in two passes. Ground of fresh propolis & turpentine, drawn from the comb in April. Top coats of cooked colophony with a measure of madder lake, brushed under a north light, dried beneath unbleached cotton. Six coats. Eight to twelve weeks.

From the Forester

The slow growth of our better spruce — sometimes thirty-five rings to the inch — cannot be compelled. We have over the last decade tightened the harvest beyond what the law requires; the felled stand of Val di Fiemme MMXXIV drew from trees seeded in the reign of Maria Theresa. To every member workshop: be sparing with the offcut. The Guild’s holdings of tonewood are intended to outlast all of us.

— Erland Holm, Guild Forester

The Maple Billet — Flat-Sawn vs. Quarter-Sawn

Examine each cut, then choose accordingly for back, ribs, or scroll.

Quarter-sawn — stiffer, prized for backs & scrolls.
Flat-sawn — cathedral grain; favoured for ribs & lesser backs.

The wood is older than the maker.
