May · MMXXVI

The ‘Maréchal’ Theorbo

A nine-course neck and fourteen-course double-headed pegbox; ivory-pointed rose carved in the shop on a single afternoon in autumn 1672; the bowl of pear-wood ribs alternated with ebony fillets.

Provenance Chain

  1. Built 1672 in the Bologna shop of Magno Tieffenbrucker (the Younger) for the household of Cardinal Pamphili.
  2. Passed to the Maréchal de Bellefonds, Paris, c. 1684, by gift of the same Cardinal.
  3. Recorded in the inventory of the Hôtel de Mailly, 1739.
  4. Reportedly held by the firm Cousineau (Paris) between c. 1788 and 1812.
  5. Acquired by Auguste Tolbecque, Niort, 1879; restored by the same.
  6. Entered the collection of the Conservatoire de Lyon, 1924, where it remains.
  7. Registered with the Worshipful Guild of Stringed-Instrument Luthiers, May MMXXVI.
