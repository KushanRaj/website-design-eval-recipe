Established MDCCXIV London · Cremona

A preface from the Warden — Three centuries gone, on a winter morning in the year of Our Lord seventeen hundred and fourteen, eleven luthiers met above a coffeehouse in Threadneedle Street and there set their mark beneath a brief Charter. They were makers of viols and viols-da-gamba, of theorbos and bowed psalteries, and they pledged to one another the care of their craft — its tools, its woods, its secrets, and the long instruction of those who would follow. That Charter, copied in eleven hands and bound in calf, remains in the Hall today.

Since that morning the Guild has weathered fires, wars, revolutions in taste and the long slow industrial wind that flattened so many trades into mere manufacture. We did not flatten. The bench is still the bench. A maple billet still asks the same patient questions of the maker; the varnish still wants the same low northern sun. Our membership now numbers one hundred and forty workshops across Europe and the Americas, every one bound to the same standards of materia, methodus, and mensura.

This folio is offered as a window onto our work. Here you will find the directory of member workshops, the formal path of the Apprenticeship, our published lore on woods and varnishes, the record of the Annual Exhibition, the Instrument of the Month, and the Journal of Techniques — our quarterly bulletin of refinements, errata, and the long arguments that keep a craft alive.

Whether you come as a young hand seeking the bench, a patron tracing the lineage of an instrument lately come to you, or a fellow maker arriving from across the sea, you are welcomed in. Read slowly. Mind the footnotes. The work is long.

— Margery St. Aubin, Warden, MMXXVI

Instrument of the Month · May MMXXVI

The ‘Maréchal’ Theorbo — Bologna, 1672

After Magno Tieffenbrucker; brought into the Guild’s registry by way of the Conservatoire de Lyon. A nine-course neck, fourteen-course double-headed pegbox, and an ivory-pointed rose carved on the orchard floor of the Tieffenbrucker shop. Its varnish, a slow amber with traces of dragon’s blood, retains a quiet glow beneath modern light.

— The Guild Oath —
I shall keep the bench clean, the wood true,
and the name of my Master honoured before mine own;
I shall teach what was given me, plainly and without reserve;
and I shall not pass off the work of my hand for what it is not.
