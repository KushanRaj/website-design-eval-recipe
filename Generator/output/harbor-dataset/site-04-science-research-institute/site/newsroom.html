Lead feature · Field dispatch

The strange quiet of the deep chorus — and what it might mean

Aboard R/V Bartholomew Strait on her 24-B leg through the Gulf of Maine basins, chief scientist Lila Marsoom files a long dispatch on a winter she did not expect.

Two of the eight low-frequency hydrophones on the Stellwagen array recorded fewer right whale upcalls in February 2026 than at any point since the array's commissioning a decade ago. Marsoom and her postdoc Henrik Larsson-Bø are not yet ready to call it a population signal — but they are ready to talk about what it would take to know.

By Lila Marsoom · 18 May 2026 · 8 min read

Okafor and Renaud share AGU's Maurice Ewing Medal

The American Geophysical Union has named Yuki Okafor and Tomás Renaud co-recipients of the 2026 Ewing Medal, recognizing four decades of foundational work in abyssal acoustic methods and benthic geochemistry, respectively.
