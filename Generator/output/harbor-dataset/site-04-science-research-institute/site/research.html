Observatory/Research & Labs
Research at Meridian

Five disciplines, seventeen labs, one continuously observed shelf

Last review · February 2026 · Maintained by the Research Office

Meridian's research is organized into five disciplines, each with two to four labs and access to a shared facility pool. We commit to long, uninterrupted sensor records — some now in their fifth decade — and to publishing the methods, the messy intermediate data, and the people behind both.

01 / 05

Coastal Dynamics

Tides, plumes, internal waves, and storm-pulse exchange on the Gulf of Maine shelf. Our coastal teams operate the Holloway Array, the Wilkinson glider line, and a fleet of acoustic Doppler current profilers.

Discipline lead · Prof. Helena Ríos-Tamayo · 4 labs · 22 scientists
CD-01

Shelf-Edge Exchange Lab

PI · Helena Ríos-Tamayo

Cross-shelf transports of heat, salt, and tracer constituents, with a focus on slope-water intrusions through the Northeast Channel.

Instruments: ADCP × 14 · CTD rosette · 2 Slocum gliders · HF radar (Cape Holloway)
CD-02

Estuarine & Plume Dynamics

PI · Marcus Tashjian

Fate and transport of riverine plumes from the Penobscot and Kennebec, including sediment, nutrients, and dissolved organic matter.

Instruments: turbidity moorings · drifters · UAS hyperspectral
CD-03

Wave & Surge Lab

PI · Devorah Linn-Karras

Storm-driven inundation, refraction across the shelf, and the slow rebuild of coastal protective sediments. Partners with the Maine State Climatologist.

Instruments: wave buoy array · pressure sensors · model coupling (FVCOM)
CD-04

Internal Wave Group

PI · Anand Wickramaratna

Internal tides, solitons, and shelf-break mixing identified through long-term thermistor strings and shipboard ADCP transects.

Instruments: thermistor moorings × 8 · upward-looking ADCPs
02 / 05

Benthic Ecology

Life on, in, and just above the seafloor — from sponge gardens at Cashes Ledge to soft-sediment communities of Wilkinson Basin. Our benthic groups maintain the longest continuous infaunal sampling program on the western Atlantic shelf.

Discipline lead · Dr. Renata Vásquez-Pernot · 3 labs · 18 scientists
BE-01

Sponge & Hard-Bottom Ecology

PI · Renata Vásquez-Pernot

Distribution and resilience of structure-forming sponges, with a long ROV survey record at Cashes Ledge dating to 1991.

Instruments: ROV Nereus II · stereo imaging sled · 3D photogrammetry
BE-02

Soft-Sediment Communities

PI · Jin-Ho Park

Infaunal time series at fixed Gulf of Maine stations; metabarcoding of macrofaunal assemblages and long-term abundance shifts.

Instruments: van Veen grabs · multicorer · benthic chambers
BE-03

Deep Reef & Coral Garden Project

PI · Sara Achebe-Lund

Mapping mesophotic and cold-water coral habitats along the Northeast Channel and Georges Bank; conservation partnerships.

Instruments: AUV Solstice · multibeam · in-situ flux chambers
BE-04

Microbiome & Sediment Geochemistry

PI · Petra Eskola

Microbial assemblages and biogeochemical gradients of the surficial mud column across the Gulf's basins.

Instruments: porewater extractors · O₂ microelectrodes · genome sequencing
03 / 05

Acoustics & Bioacoustics

Ocean sound as data: how it travels, what it carries, and who is making it. Our hydrophone arrays produce one of the largest continuous acoustic records in the western North Atlantic.

Discipline lead · Dr. Lila Marsoom · 3 labs · 14 scientists
AB-01

Marine Mammal Acoustics

PI · Lila Marsoom

Detection and classification of baleen whale vocalizations across the Stellwagen and Cape Cod arrays. Quiet-shipping advocacy through NOAA partnership.

Instruments: Stellwagen array · DMON tags · convolutional detectors
AB-02

Soundscape & Anthrophony

PI · Eitan Volkov

Long-term spectral statistics of the soundscape and the shipping contribution to low-frequency ambient noise.

Instruments: passive hydrophone moorings × 12 · OpenAcoustics pipeline
AB-03

Active Acoustics & Tomography

PI · Sumiko Ozaki

Reciprocal acoustic tomography over the Maine slope; method development for low-power, long-duration tomographic transmissions.

Instruments: tomographic sources · clock-disciplined moorings
04 / 05

Ocean Chemistry

Carbonate chemistry, nutrient cycling, and persistent contaminants. Our chemistry labs maintain monthly hydrographic sampling at Holloway Station and the offshore Wilkinson Time-Series.

Discipline lead · Dr. Ihsan El-Maktoum · 3 labs · 19 scientists
OC-01

Carbonate & Acidification Lab

PI · Ihsan El-Maktoum

pH, DIC, and alkalinity at fixed stations; ocean acidification trends across the Maine shelf with a 22-year record.

Instruments: VINDTA · spectrophotometric pH · SAMI-CO₂ moorings
OC-02

Nutrient & Productivity Group

PI · Marit Sigurðardóttir

Continuous nutrient monitoring, phytoplankton community structure, and the spring bloom's annual choreography.

Instruments: SUNA nitrate · FRRf · imaging FlowCytobot
OC-03

Contaminants & Microplastics

PI · Tomás Renaud

Long-term legacy contaminant burdens in sediments and biota, and emerging questions on microplastic vertical distribution.

Instruments: GC-MS · ICP-MS · μ-FTIR
05 / 05

Climate & Cryosphere

The subpolar gyre, the freshening of the Labrador Sea, and the downstream consequences for Gulf of Maine waters. Long observational moorings, paleo records, and coupled climate modeling.

Discipline lead · Prof. Joaquim Selvarajan · 4 labs · 16 scientists
CC-01

Subpolar Mooring Group

PI · Joaquim Selvarajan

The Labrador Mooring Line — operated continuously since 2003 — provides daily transport estimates of the boundary current.

Instruments: 8-mooring array · MicroCAT-IDOs · current meters
CC-02

Paleoceanography & Sediment Cores

PI · Nadia Olszewska-Brent

High-resolution Holocene records of North Atlantic variability from sediment cores; isotopic and micropaleontological methods.

Instruments: piston cores · XRF scanner · stable isotope lab
CC-03

Sea Ice & Cryosphere Interactions

PI · Aksel Bø Halvorsen

Coupled modeling of marginal ice zone processes; collaboration with NSIDC and the Norwegian Polar Institute.

Instruments: ICESat-2 retrieval · ROMS-CICE configurations
CC-04

Climate Diagnostics & Reanalysis

PI · Patrice Côté-Yamamoto

Reanalysis-based diagnostics of subpolar variability; participates in CMIP intercomparison and OceanSODA.

Instruments: HPC cluster · ERA5 · GLORYS · in-house emulators

Shared Facilities

Cross-cutting infrastructure available to all Meridian labs and to qualifying external users through our Berth-on-Cruise and Facility Access programs.

Operations Office · access@meridian-obs.org
FAC-A

Mooring & Buoy Engineering

Manager · Carl Eriksson

Design, deployment, and recovery of long-term sensor moorings — from coastal buoys to abyssal anchor lines.

Capability: 32 active moorings · in-house calibration tank
FAC-B

Stable Isotope Facility

Manager · Lakshmi Vembanad

δ¹³C, δ¹⁵N, δ¹⁸O for water, sediment, and biota; community service rates for partner institutions.

Equipment: 3 IRMS · EA-CF coupling · prep-line redundancy
FAC-C

ROV & AUV Operations

Manager · Etienne Mbaye

Mission planning and pilot services for ROV Nereus II (4500 m) and AUV Solstice for the institute and qualifying users.

Vehicles: ROV Nereus II · AUV Solstice · 2 Slocum gliders
FAC-D

Holloway Pier & Vessels

Manager · Jess Boudreau

R/V Bartholomew Strait (58 m, global), R/V Thrush (28 m, regional), small-boat fleet, and the working pier.

Capacity: 24 berths offshore · 8 berths nearshore
