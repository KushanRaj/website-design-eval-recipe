Dr. Yuki Okafor

Director · Senior Scientist, Climate & Cryosphere

Yuki Okafor has led Meridian since 2021. Her research uses long, uninterrupted mooring records to extract decadal change signals from the subpolar Atlantic — quietly, with statistical conservatism, and in close collaboration with the engineers who keep the moorings on the line.

Before becoming director, Okafor ran the Subpolar Mooring Group for a decade and was lead PI on the OSNAP-W Long Line program. Her work on the Labrador Mooring Line provides one of the world's few daily-resolved transport estimates of a deep boundary current, and she has been a vocal advocate for treating maintenance of such records as a first-order scientific contribution.

She serves on the U.S. CLIVAR Subpolar North Atlantic Working Group, co-chairs the OceanObs'29 organizing committee, and is a Fellow of the American Geophysical Union (2023).

Current projects

What I am working on now

OSNAP-W Long Line (2003 – present). Continuing daily transport estimation; intercomparison with OSNAP-E and Florida Strait records; an open transport product released annually.

Subpolar Reorganization Study (2024 – 2027). Quantifying the apparent slowdown signal in the Labrador Current and its propagation through the slope sea. NSF-funded, joint with Université Laval.

Long Records Reuse Initiative. An institute-wide effort to publish every continuous Meridian time series at submission-friendly, citation-ready resolution by 2027.
