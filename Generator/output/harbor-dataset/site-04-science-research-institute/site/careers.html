CULT

Working at the Observatory

Meridian's 142 people work from a single coastal campus on Cape Holloway, Maine. The pace is intentional: long-record science requires staffing for steady, multi-decadal stewardship, not bursty project cycles. Most staff take sea time as part of normal duties, and we maintain a culture of careful documentation and shared maintenance.

Director's Office · culture@meridian-obs.org

Location & campus

A working pier, four research buildings, a wet-lab annex, and a residential cottage cluster on a forested point above the Gulf of Maine. Sixty minutes from Bangor International by road.

Housing

Subsidized institute housing for first-year staff and a rolling sublet network coordinated by the People Office.

Sea time

Every research-track position includes scheduled sea days. Most administrative and engineering positions also rotate aboard at least once per year by choice.

Sabbatical & leave

Sabbatical eligibility for permanent scientists after seven years; generous parental and bereavement leave; institutionally supported caregiving stipends for sea-going parents.

BEN

Benefits

Standard institutional benefits package, with several details specific to our coastal, sea-going posture.

people@meridian-obs.org

Health & family

Full medical/dental/vision; mental-health concierge; 20 weeks of paid parental leave; tuition support for dependents.

Retirement

11% employer contribution to TIAA-equivalent plan after one year; no vesting cliff.

Sea allowance

Per-diem and gear stipend for all sea-going staff; supplemental insurance during expeditions.

Professional development

USD 4,500 annual development budget for all permanent staff; full conference and society membership support for research-track scientists.

DEI

Equity, Diversity & Accessibility

We are committed to a workforce that reflects the communities the ocean touches. We recognize that field science has historically been inaccessible by design, and we are working — concretely, with budgets — to change that.

Our practices include name-blinded first-pass review for all faculty and postdoctoral searches, accessibility audits of the Bartholomew Strait and Thrush on a four-year cycle, captioned dispatches, and a sea-going caregivers' fund that has supported 18 staff members since 2022.

Read our 2026 DEI plan · dei@meridian-obs.org
