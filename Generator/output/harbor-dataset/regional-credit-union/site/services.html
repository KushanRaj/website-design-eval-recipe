V.
Glossary & Marginalia

Words we use plainly, words we hold loosely.

APR vs APY

APR — annual percentage rate — is what you pay on a loan over a year. APY — annual percentage yield — is what you earn on a deposit over a year, with compounding included. Two letters; opposite sides of the ledger.

Share vs Deposit

A commercial bank holds your deposits. A credit union holds your shares. The five dollars in your share savings account isn't owed to you — it's the ownership stake that makes you an owner.

Dividend vs Interest

Dividends are paid after the cooperative covers its obligations: a share of surplus, by member vote. Commercial interest is paid before profit to shareholders.

Patronage Refund

An end-of-year return on your share of cooperative business done. Three Rivers issued $2.1M in patronage refunds in 2025 — averaging $24.96 per active member.
