BALLOT OPEN

Cast your 2026 board ballot.

Five candidates are standing for three open seats. Voting is ranked-choice and requires your member number plus your online banking password.

Ballot closes at 11:59 p.m. on June 13. The Inspector of Elections certifies results live at the annual meeting the next evening.

Member Proposals

Submit a proposal for the annual meeting.

Any member in good standing may submit a written proposal for consideration at the annual meeting. Proposals must be received at least 60 days in advance. (We'll work on yours before then.)

Past member proposals include: weekend hours at Riverbend (1989, adopted), a community-solar line of credit (2014, adopted), and a board-term-limit amendment (2019, defeated).

The Seven Rochdale Principles, plainly stated.

Adopted by the International Cooperative Alliance and observed by every credit union worth the name. We re-publish them at every annual meeting, in tiny caps at the bottom of every page.

  1. Voluntary & Open Membership. Anyone within our field of membership may join — without political, religious, or social discrimination.
  2. Democratic Member Control. One member, one vote, regardless of share balance.
  3. Member Economic Participation. Members contribute equitably to, and democratically control, the capital of the cooperative.
  4. Autonomy & Independence. Cooperatives are self-help organizations controlled by their members; outside agreements preserve that autonomy.
  5. Education, Training, & Information. We educate our members so they can contribute effectively — hence the Reading Room.
  6. Cooperation Among Cooperatives. We work with other co-ops — and lend to them.
  7. Concern for Community. Sustainable development of communities through policies approved by the membership.
