Sable
Circuit

Hometown · Brooklyn, NY Formed · 2018 Members · K. Park (vocals, guitar) · J. Ohan (bass) · F. Liddell (drums) · M. Reyes (synth) Tags · post-punk / noise On Xerox · since 2020

Sable Circuit started as two friends in a Bushwick basement with a borrowed bass amp and a four-track cassette deck. Six years later they are a four-piece who tour with their own PA, refuse to play anything before 11pm, and have somehow convinced four different pressing plants that their music is not a malfunction.

The band's third record, Concrete Lullaby, is the densest thing they have ever pressed — eight songs recorded live to half-inch tape in a Greenpoint boiler room across six consecutive nights in February.1 Guitars are detuned to F. Drums are hit with felt mallets. The synth is the same Korg MS-20 that appeared on every previous Sable Circuit record, except this time it is broken in a new way.

"The most quietly furious record on the label this year."
— Pressing Notes №42

What separates Sable Circuit from the rest of the New York post-punk class is that they do not write songs for the song. They write songs for the room — for the specific way feedback wraps a brick wall, for the way a kick drum lands in a low ceiling. Concrete Lullaby is a record that knows what a basement sounds like.

K. Park's lyrics on this record are noticeably less abstract than the band's first two LPs. Where Tar Pit Theatre (XRX-022) was dense with metaphor about labor and exhaustion, Concrete Lullaby names places, friends, and city blocks directly.2 The album opens with a song called "Withers Street Vigil" and closes with one called "Crossing Manhattan At 4am In A Borrowed Truck."

Tape is honest. Tape doesn't forgive you. We needed both.

The band recorded Concrete Lullaby at Pressing Plant 3, the studio side of the label, with engineer R. Salinas at the desk. The plates were cut by hand in-house and shipped to the Brooklyn warehouse for inspection. The first pressing of the album — 150 copies on pink marble and 250 on cyan splatter — sold out the night the pre-order opened. A second pressing is going up Friday at noon.

Live, the band is louder than the record. They are touring the East Coast and the UK this summer with support from labelmates Mara Vex & The Hex and Annex 47. Booking inquiries go to booker@xeroxpressing.co — please include the room capacity and a sound system spec sheet.

"They sound like a basement remembering itself."

If you have not seen Sable Circuit in a small room, that is the thing to fix first. The records are very good. The records are also a souvenir from a place you should go to. The place is somewhere with a ceiling that is six and a half feet tall, a power strip that is overheating, and a beer that is room temperature. We will see you there.
