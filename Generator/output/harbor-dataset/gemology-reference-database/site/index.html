Gem of the Week — May 2026

Kashmir Sapphire

The Padparadscha of velvety blues — Kashmir sapphires command extraordinary premiums at auction for their intensely saturated, cornflower-blue hue with a characteristic velvety, sleepy appearance caused by dense silk needle inclusions. First discovered in 1881 in the Zanskar Range, the alluvial deposits were largely exhausted within a decade, making genuine Kashmir provenance among the most coveted in all of gemology.

A fine, unheated Kashmir sapphire can exceed $200,000 per carat — surpassing many comparable-quality Burmese rubies — driven by extreme rarity and the prestige attached to the origin.
