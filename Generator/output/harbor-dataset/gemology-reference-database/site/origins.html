Notable Deposit Narratives

The Mogok Stone Tract, Myanmar

For over a millennium, the Mogok Valley has yielded the world's finest rubies — the chromium-rich marble metamorphics creating conditions for the vivid "Pigeon's Blood" coloration unmatched elsewhere. The Valley of Rubies stretches 400 km² and also produces fine blue and violet sapphires, red spinel (historically confused with ruby — the "Black Prince's Ruby" is in fact spinel), and moon stones.

Muzo, Colombia — The Emerald Capital

The Muzo mines in Boyacá, Colombia have been worked since pre-Columbian times. Colombian emeralds are distinguished by their inclusions of three-phase fluid inclusions (brine + gas bubble + salt crystal), confirming hydrothermal origin in black bituminous shale. The characteristic slightly bluish-green "Colombian green" is considered the world standard for emerald color.

Kashmir — The Lost Mines

Discovered in 1881 after a landslide revealed blue crystals in the Zanskar Range at 4,000m altitude, the Kashmir sapphire deposits were essentially exhausted by 1888. A second period of mining 1906–1927 recovered additional material. The characteristic "velvety" appearance — caused by dense rutile silk inclusions — has never been fully replicated in any other deposit, making genuine Kashmir origin the most valuable provenance in sapphire.
