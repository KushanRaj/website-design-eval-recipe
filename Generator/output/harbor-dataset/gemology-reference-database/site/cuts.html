Editor's Feature

The Round Brilliant

Developed by Marcel Tolkowsky in 1919, the Round Brilliant cut with 57 or 58 facets (with or without culet) remains the most scientifically optimized cut for maximum fire and brilliance in diamond. Tolkowsky's calculated crown angle of 34.5° and pavilion angle of 40.75° established the proportions that still define "ideal cut" today.

The cut's evolution from the earlier Old European and Old Mine cuts represents a century of optical engineering — modern computer-modeled variants such as the "Super Ideal" push fire and scintillation metrics further while maintaining light performance.
