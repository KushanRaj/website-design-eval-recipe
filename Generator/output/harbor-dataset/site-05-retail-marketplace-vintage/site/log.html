Featured · May 12, 2026 · Polk County, IA

"Three generations, one barn, and a ledger of every chair in the house."

The yard sign was hand-painted in fence paint, the driveway was lined with somebody's cousins, and the grandkids — Lily, who runs the local feed store, and Bo, who'd driven down from Cedar Rapids — handed me a Mead composition book before I'd even gotten out of the truck.

It was a ledger. Every chair in the house, going back to 1948. Which uncle scuffed the slipper in the den. Which cat threw up on the rocker. Which year the bachelor great-uncle moved the apothecary cabinet from the porch to the dining room because he was tired of looking at it through the screen door.

I read it for forty minutes before I touched anything. Then I bought eleven lots. The mustard slipper chair you'll see Thursday at noon is the one in the photo above, marked here with hotspot 1 — yes, the same chair the ledger says smoked a Don Tomás for four decades.
