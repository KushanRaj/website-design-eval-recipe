Chapter 1 — Field of Play

Ice Sheet Dimensions

The playing surface (the sheet) shall be rectangular, measuring 45.720 metres in length and 4.750 metres in width, bounded by sideboards and including a tee line, hog lines, back lines, and centre line. Where ice conditions permit, sheet width may be reduced to 4.420 metres for non-championship play with the Chief Ice Technician's written approval.

Fig. 1.1 — Standard ice sheet with house markings and lines.

House Markings

Each house consists of four concentric circles centred on the tee, with outer diameters of 12 ft (3.66 m), 8 ft (2.44 m), 4 ft (1.22 m), and the button at 1 ft (0.30 m). All stones whose lower edge lies on or inside the outer ring at the conclusion of the end are eligible to count, subject to §4.1.

Hog & Back Lines

Hog lines are positioned 11.28 m from each tee. A stone whose leading edge does not fully cross the far hog line shall be removed from play immediately and shall not affect any other stone in motion. The back line passes through the rear edge of the back rings of each house.

Chapter 2 — Equipment

Stones (Granite)

Each stone shall be of approved quarried granite, weighing between 17.24 kg and 19.96 kg inclusive of handle. The running surface shall measure 12.7 cm in diameter with a concave profile and a band finish per WCF specification GR-4. The cross-section is illustrated in Fig. 2.1.

Fig. 2.1 — Granite stone cross-section.

Brushes & Heads

Approved brush heads are listed in equipment bulletin EQ-2026-04. Use of unapproved or modified pad materials shall result in removal of the offending player for the remainder of the end, with the opposing skip's election under §6.4.

Rule Change · Effective 01 June 2026
Three new pad models (P-Glide M5, IsoFib RX, Cascade Pro 18) added to the approved list. The Helix-2 model is withdrawn pending re-testing. Officials shall enforce the updated list at all NCF-sanctioned competitions and recognised club leagues.

Footwear & Sliders

One slider per athlete shall be worn during delivery on the foot opposite the throwing hand. Sliders affixed with reflective or contrasting tape for officiating visibility are permitted; mechanical assistance devices remain prohibited under §6.1(c).

Chapter 3 — Play of the Game

Coin Toss & Hammer

Prior to the first end, the Chief Umpire shall conduct a coin toss; the winner elects either to take last-stone advantage (the "hammer") in the first end or choose stone colour. In tournament play following a round-robin, hammer is determined by the team with the lower DSC value.

Delivery

A delivery is deemed complete when the stone is released from the hand of the delivering player. If a stone is released after touching the near hog line, it is a hog and must be removed before reaching the playing area.

Sweeping

Two sweepers from the delivering team may sweep their own stone anywhere between the tee lines on either side of the sheet, provided their brush heads do not pass directly over a stationary stone. Opposing-team sweeping behind the tee line is permitted on one stone per end at the skip's discretion, in accordance with §3.3(b).

Free Guard Zone

Stones at rest between the hog line and the tee line, outside the house, are protected from removal until five stones have been delivered in the end (the so-called "5-rock rule"). Stones removed in contravention shall be replaced to their pre-violation positions by the non-offending skip.

Fig. 3.1 — Free Guard Zone (shaded) between hog and tee lines, outside the house.

Chapter 4 — Scoring

Counting Stones

Only one team scores per end. That team's stones that lie closer to the tee than the nearest opposing stone shall each count one (1) point. A blank end shall be scored zero, and the hammer is retained per §3.1.

DSC & Tiebreaks

The Draw-Shot Challenge (DSC) is conducted prior to round-robin play: each team delivers one stone to the button; the distance from the tee determines seed-order ranking. In the event of identical records, head-to-head outcomes shall be applied before DSC, per §4.7.

Chapter 5 — Officiating

Chief Umpire

The Chief Umpire, appointed for each NCF-sanctioned event, holds final on-ice authority and may delegate to Deputy Umpires for individual sheets. The Chief Umpire shall be a current Level 4 or Level 5 certified official.

Measurements

Measurements may be requested only after the final stone of the end has come to rest. A six-foot measuring stick is the standard implement; verniers and electronic measures are permitted at all NCF Championship events.

Protests

Written protests shall be submitted to the Chief Umpire no later than thirty (30) minutes following the conclusion of the contested end. Protests regarding ice conditions shall be heard jointly with the Chief Ice Technician.

Chapter 6 — Conduct & Sanctions

Players, coaches, and team officials shall conduct themselves at all times in a manner consistent with the spirit of the sport. Verbal or physical abuse, equipment misuse, or unsporting delay shall be sanctioned by warning, removal for the end, removal for the match, or further sanctions referred to the National Disciplinary Panel under §6.6.

Chapter 7 — Certification Ladder
